A Simple Data-Augmented Framework For Smoothed Recommender System

Anonymous Author  

1. Abstract

Recommender System holds the promise of predicting users preferences based on their historical behaviours. However, most existing models suffer from the over-fitting issue in practice due to the ubiquitous sparsity problem in real-world datasets, which result in less robust without additional augmented samples. To alleviate this problem, in this paper, we propose a simple yet effective recommender system framework to obtain a smoothed model and improve the generalization performance. The idea is to generate a set of augmented samples from a tailored Gaussian distribution, and then estimate the models parameters via an adversarial training. To understand that, we devise two types of generating process called dataand perturbation-oriented methods respectively. The former is able to exploit the underlying diversity of original data points, while the latter aims at maximizing diversity of perturbation. Beyond that, we provide a theoretical analysis on the optimization objective and introduce a gradient-norm regularization for encouraging smoothness in the fine-tunning phase. Extensive experiments on six real-world datasets with six baselines demonstrate our frameworks effectiveness and generality.

3. Our Framework.

Data-oriented method: Generating a set of augmented samples from Gaussian distribution with mean x ,i.e., the original data point, for each data point.

Perturbation-oriented method: Generating a set of augmented samples from Gaussian distribution with mean eta ,i.e., the perturbation, for each data point.

Adversarial Training method: A simple yet effective adversarial training process to estimate the parameters of model.

Contributions

  • We propose a simple yet effective framework to empower recommendation models with non-random perturbations data. To the best of our knowledge, our work is the first attempt to leverage the idea of adversarial learning in the fields of smoothed recommendation domain.
  • We implement the above idea in two ways, developing a data-oriented and perturbation-oriented to generate augmented data points, and then by employing an adversarial training to against random perturbation.
  • We theoretically analyze the smoothness regularization in the objective and introduce a gradient-norm regularization to the original objective.
  • We conduct extensive experiments based on six real-world datasets and six baselines to verify the superiority of our framework.
